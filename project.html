Project

1. Research

These are some ideas I wanted to work on/got inspired by. I use the site Instructables to find inspiration. Many people who have done a variety of projects upload their works there. I'm interested in making a light that has a sensor, such that when you place your hand on it, it lights up.


Inspo 1: Eclipse Lamp
An eclipse lamp seemed like a cool way to turn on a light. I thought the prototyping process for the gears and points where the lights turn on and off would be interesting to play around with.


Inspo 2: Mood Lamp
Was highly considering this, but I was wondering if it was possible to do it as a mini “streetlamp” instead. This tutorial in particular made use of a recycled lamp, electronics are also not included.


After some research, it was clear to me my design mind was getting to me, I needed to look more for the functionalities and electronics of the project rather than the designs. So I started researching electronics on using sensors to turn on a light inside a lamp. This is what I found.


Tutorial 1: Light Sensor (Photoresistor)
This tutorial was done using Arduino in Tinkercad. Tutorial 2: Another Light Sensor with Arduino I am more inclined to using this tutorial as I feel it would fit better with the project I am planning to do. I also felt that it was simpler. Being a beginner, I felt this would be a safe route to go. Regardless, these are my two options.

I also settled on the tutorial I wanted to follow for my lamp. Right here
This is a silhoutte lamp. Depending on the way you choose to cut an image, a shape will be projected on the box when the light is turned on. I intend to make a silhoutte lamp that turns on when you place your hand on it.
